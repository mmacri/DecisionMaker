Phase 3 · Six-phase CSIR workflow

Aligned to Preparation → Identification → Investigation → Response → Recovery → Follow Up.

Animated workflow

Preparation — readiness checks, roster validation.
Identification — event vs. incident determination and severity tag.
Investigation — evidence capture, containment options.
Response — communication matrix and escalation rules.
Recovery — service restoration and validation steps.
Follow Up — DOE-417/E-ISAC reporting, lessons learned.

Severity alignment

Critical

Major operational disruption or safety risk. Immediate Incident Commander activation.

High

Significant impact requiring Technical Lead coordination and rapid reporting checks.

Medium

Contained or localized issues with targeted response and documentation.

Low

Minor anomalies tracked for trend analysis and awareness reinforcement.

Phase 4 · Role-based training paths

Each path bundles the foundation, then layers role-specific guidance, simulations, and downloads.

All SCL Staff (General Awareness)

5–7 minutes · scenario-based questions.

  • Recognize abnormalities and report promptly.
  • Know who to contact and when.
  • Annual training attestation and quick contact card download.

CSIR Team Member

10–12 minutes · interactive workflow simulation.

  • Deep dive across all six phases.
  • Documentation, communication, and checklists.
  • Coordination scenarios with immediate feedback.

Technical Lead

15–18 minutes · complex branching decisions.

  • Investigation leadership and severity determination.
  • Escalation timing and containment playbooks.
  • Technical decision-making practice.

Incident Commander

15–18 minutes · leadership simulations.

  • Team coordination and documentation oversight.
  • Stakeholder communication matrix and ICS triggers.
  • Post-incident follow-up and lessons learned facilitation.

CSIR Reporter (Advanced)

20–25 minutes · compliance-focused scenarios.

  • Reportability determination and DOE-417 completion.
  • E-ISAC/CISA timelines, AtC vs. reportable distinctions.
  • Logging requirements and evidence handoff.

Phase 5 · Scenario-based assessment

Branching simulations mirror real alerts and enforce correct workflow sequencing.

Sample scenario — Unexpected login alert

  1. Initial response: classify as cyber event vs. incident with rationale.
  2. Severity assessment: choose Low/Medium/High/Critical and see impact feedback.
  3. Notification: verify who to contact based on severity and role.
  4. Outcome: correct path displayed with remediation and reporting timers.

Assessment engine

  • Timed decision points (optional pressure mode).
  • Score tracking and must-pass knowledge checks.
  • Immediate remediation guidance for wrong turns.
  • Resume where you left off; progress bar visible throughout.
